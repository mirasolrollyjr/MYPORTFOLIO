Second Quarter Activities

  • Buwan ng Wika

    What I learned:
I learned to appreciate our Filipino language and culture more deeply. The event reminded me of the importance of using Filipino with pride and respect.

Application in real life:
I can use what I learned by speaking Filipino correctly and promoting our culture through everyday conversations and school activities.

Participation:
I joined our class presentation and we did the monologue for the performance task in Filipino. We watched in the auditorium the debate and other grade levels—it was fun and I wondered how they memorized those long pieces.

How I would explain it:
Buwan ng Wika celebrates our national language and identity, encouraging students to value their roots.

Importance of the event:
It helps strengthen our sense of nationalism and reminds us of the beauty of our own language.

  • Intrams

    What I learned:
I learned the value of teamwork, sportsmanship, and discipline through friendly competition.

Application in real life:
I can apply this by cooperating with others and accepting both success and failure positively.

Participation:
I joined the Mobile Legends and Basketball games — we won 1st place in Mobile Legends but sadly lost in basketball, even when I scored 25+ points. Although we lost, game is game; we need to learn discipline and control our emotions.

How I would explain it:
Intramurals is a school event that promotes unity and physical fitness through different sports and contests.

Importance of the event:
It encourages healthy competition and builds camaraderie among students.

  • Science Month

    What I learned:
I learned how science applies to everyday life and how innovation can solve real-world problems.

Application in real life:
I can use this by being curious, asking questions, and thinking of ways to help the environment.

Participation:
I participated in the Nutri Chant, and our class got the 2nd highest in our grade level. I also made a poster about innovation.
We also presented our research proposal and did well.

How I would explain it:
Science Month aims to inspire students to explore scientific ideas and appreciate the role of technology in society.

Importance of the event:
It develops curiosity and critical thinking skills that are important for lifelong learning.

  • Araling Panlipunan (AP) Month

    What I learned:
During AP Month, I learned more about our country’s history and the importance of defending our national sovereignty. 
We attended a seminar discussing the West Philippine Sea issue, where I understood how it affects our country’s rights and territorial integrity. 
The event helped me see the value of patriotism and being informed about current national issues.

Application in real life:
I can apply what I learned by staying aware of national issues and supporting peaceful ways to protect our country’s interests.

Participation:
I joined the seminar about KADA and represented our school. I also recited during the seminar.

How I would explain it:
AP Month promotes love for the country and helps students understand civic duties, cultural identity, and current national issues.

Importance of the event:
It teaches students to appreciate the sacrifices of our heroes, value our sovereignty, and stay informed about the challenges our nation faces today.

  • Teacher’s Day

    What I learned:
I learned to appreciate the hard work, patience, and dedication of our teachers.

Application in real life:
I can apply this by showing respect, gratitude, and cooperation in every class.

Participation:
I helped prepare the mass and a surprise for our adviser with roses.

How I would explain it:
Teachers’ Day is a celebration that honors the efforts and sacrifices of our educators.

Importance of the event:
It reminds students to value education and the people who guide us to success.
