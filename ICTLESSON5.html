ðŸ’¡ Introduction

In this lesson, I learned how **CSS selectors** are used to target and style specific HTML elements. There are different types of selectors, such as class, ID, element, group, universal, and attribute selectors. Each selector has a unique way of applying styles depending on the element or condition.

ðŸ“˜ Lesson Summary

I learned that CSS selectors are the foundation of styling in web design. The **element selector** targets all occurrences of a specific tag, while the **ID selector** (using `#`) styles a unique element. The **class selector** (using `.`) applies styles to multiple elements that share the same class. The **universal selector** (`*`) styles all elements at once, while **group selectors** combine several elements to share one style. Lastly, the **attribute selector** targets elements based on specific attributes or their values.
